Mushin (無心) Privacy Policy

Last updated: 2026-07-02

In short...

Mushin stores the account and activity data needed to run your record: your username or guest account, your activities, entries, notes, comments, connections, and account settings. You can export that data, replace it by importing a Mushin export file, or delete it. Deleting your account removes the data owned by that account, and inactive guest accounts are removed automatically on a retention schedule.

This policy explains what AQNAS collects for the Mushin service, how it is used, when it is deleted, and what controls you have over it.

1. What we collect

We collect the information needed to create and operate your Mushin record. What we collect depends on how you use the service.

a. Account identity and login data

  • If you create a password account, we collect your username, your password hash, and your optional recovery email address.
  • Passwords are not stored in plain text. Mushin stores only an Argon2id password hash.
  • If a deployment offers OAuth sign-in, Mushin may collect the provider account identifier and profile name needed to attach that identity to your account.
  • If you use Mushin as a guest, Mushin creates a guest account tied to the signed session cookie in your browser.

b. Account metadata

  • Your timezone setting
  • Your visibility setting
  • Account timestamps such as created-at, last-active-at, and deletion status

c. Data you create in Mushin

  • Categories and activities
  • Entries, including occurred-at timestamps, notes, tags, and numeric values
  • Match records for competition-style activities
  • Comments you write on entries
  • Connection and block records used for fellows and profile access

d. Technical and operational data

  • Standard connection metadata such as IP address, request time, and browser or device information may appear in server and proxy logs.
  • Mushin uses a signed session cookie to keep you logged in or to keep a guest account attached to your browser session.

2. How we use it

  • To create your account or guest record and keep you signed in
  • To store your activities, entries, notes, comments, and stats
  • To apply your visibility, fellow, and block settings
  • To let you export, import, or delete your data
  • To operate, secure, and troubleshoot the service

Mushin does not use your entries or notes for advertising, profiling, or third-party analytics.

3. Who can see your data

Mushin is a multi-user service, so some data can be visible to other people depending on your settings.

  • Private accounts: other people can still discover your username and activity names, but they cannot open the private activity detail pages or see your notes unless they are accepted mutual connections ("fellows").
  • Public accounts: your full record, including entries and notes, can be viewed by anyone with your profile link.
  • Fellows: accepted mutual connections can see the full record, including notes, even if the account is otherwise private.
  • Comments and connections are visible where needed for the shared social features to work.

4. Sharing and service providers

Mushin does not sell your data. We share data only when needed to run the service, comply with the law, or when you intentionally expose data through Mushin's own sharing and visibility features.

Provider Purpose Data involved
Cloudflare Traffic routing, DNS, TLS, and edge proxying Connection metadata such as IP address
Server and database host Application hosting and storage The account and activity data stored by Mushin
OAuth provider, if enabled Authentication Provider identifier and profile details used for sign-in

Mushin does not send your notes, entries, or export files to outside AI services or third-party analytics tools.

5. Retention and deletion

a. Logged-in accounts

  • Your account data is kept until you delete the account.
  • Deleting an account deletes the user row and the data that account owns through database cascade deletion, including activities, entries, notes, tags, values, matches, comments, and relationship records tied to that account.

b. Guest accounts

  • Guests can also delete their data through the in-product delete flow.
  • Mushin also removes stale guest accounts automatically. The current schedule is about 7 days for guests with no entries and about 30 days for inactive guests.

c. Logs

  • Operational logs are kept only as long as needed for security, abuse prevention, and troubleshooting, then rotated or removed.

6. Your controls

  • You can change your visibility setting in your account page.
  • You can export your current Mushin data as a JSON file from the account page.
  • You can import a Mushin export file to replace the data currently stored under your account.
  • You can delete your account or guest record from within Mushin.
  • Logging out clears the session cookie but does not delete your stored account data.

7. Contact and updates

If this policy changes, the updated version will be published at this page. Material changes will be reflected in the policy text before they take effect in the service.
