Mushin (無心) Privacy Policy

In short...

Mushin stores the activity entries you log so it can show your counts and progress. Even if you use "Get started" without an account, your record is stored on Mushin's servers, not just on your device. Anything you write in a note is never sent to any outside company or AI service. If you delete your account, your record and notes are deleted together, and guest records are automatically removed after a period of inactivity.

AQNAS ("we", "us", or "the operator") respects your privacy and is committed to protecting the personal information you share with us. This policy explains what information we collect, how we use it, and the choices you have.

This policy applies to the personal progress tracking service "Mushin" (https://mushin.aqnas.xyz, the "Service") provided by the operator.

1. Information we collect

We process personal information for everyone who uses the Service, including guests who use it without creating an account. The specific information depends on how you use the Service.

a. Account holders (social login — Google)

  • (Required) Your Google account identifier, email address, and profile name (used as your display name in the Service)
  • The scope we request from Google is openid email profile.

b. Account holders (email/password signup)

  • (Required) Email address (used as your account identifier) and password
  • Your password is never stored or logged in plain text. It is stored only as an irreversible Argon2id hash. We have no way to see your original password.

c. Guests ("Get started" without an account)

  • If you use the Service without signing up or logging in, we create an anonymous server account linked to a device identifier cookie.
  • We don't collect your name, email, or phone number this way, but everything you log — entries, notes, and other data — is stored on our servers (a SQLite database), not on your device. Guest data is personal information and is protected the same way as an account holder's data.

d. Information created while using the Service (account holders and guests alike, isolated per account)

  • The activities (categories) and sub-tallies you create
  • Entries: when something happened (occurred_at), any free-text notes you write (we treat these as potentially sensitive, since you can write anything — health details, names, locations, etc.), selected tags, and numeric values (counts, scales)
  • Match records: opponent, score, and result
  • Account activity timestamps: when your account was created (created_at) and when you were last active (last_active_at)

e. Automatically collected information

  • Standard web server access logs (IP address, access time, browser and device information, and other connection metadata) may be generated automatically as you use the Service.
  • Since our traffic passes through Cloudflare, Cloudflare may process connection metadata such as your IP address (see section 4).

2. How we use your information

We use the information we collect only for the purposes below. If that ever changes, we'll update this policy and seek your consent as required.

  1. Account identification and authentication — verifying who you are via social login or email/password, and keeping you signed in
  2. Providing the Service — storing and retrieving your entries, and powering totals, statistics, and streaks
  3. Guest mode and account switching — letting you use the Service without signing up, connecting your guest record to a real account later, and either deleting your guest data immediately or letting it expire under our retention schedule (section 3) when you switch to an existing account
  4. Operating and securing the Service — preventing abuse, troubleshooting, and maintaining security through access logs
  5. Responding to your requests — handling requests to access, correct, delete, or withdraw consent for your data

3. How long we keep your information

As a general rule, we delete personal information without delay once the purpose for collecting it has been fulfilled. The specific retention periods are below.

a. Account holders (Google, email/password)

  • Retention period: until you delete your account
  • When you delete your account, all of your data (activities, sub-tallies, tags, entries, notes, match records, and so on) is deleted along with it (see section 5).

b. Guests

  • Guests can delete their own data at any time using the delete control in the Service.
  • In addition, we run an automated retention job that removes guest data under the following schedule:
    • Guests with no entries: removed automatically about 7 days after the account is created
    • Inactive guests: removed automatically about 30 days after the last activity
  • These periods are the current defaults and may be adjusted as we operate the Service.

c. Web server access logs

  • Retention period: 30 days for operational and security purposes, or until log rotation
  • Access logs are deleted after this period.

4. Sharing and third-party processing

a. Sharing with third parties

We do not share your personal information with third parties beyond what is described in this policy, except in the following cases:

  • You have given separate, prior consent
  • Disclosure is required by law or requested by a law enforcement agency through lawful procedure

b. Service providers and third-party processing

To operate the Service reliably, we rely on the following third parties, which may process your personal information:

Provider Purpose Information processed
Google Social login (authentication) Google account identifier, email, name
Oracle Cloud Server and database hosting (data storage) All data stored by the Service
Cloudflare DNS, TLS, CDN/proxy (traffic routing) IP address and other connection metadata

c. We don't send your data to outside APIs

We never send your account data — including your notes, entries, tags, or match records — to any third-party API. Specifically:

  • We don't offer any summary or analysis features powered by external AI/LLM services,
  • We don't use third-party analytics tools, and
  • We don't use any external error-tracking tool that could log what you've typed.

If we ever introduce a feature like this in the future, we'll update this policy in advance and only proceed after obtaining your separate, explicit consent — never as a quiet addition.

The same applies to the export/import feature described in section 6: any file you download or upload is generated and processed entirely on our own server. No third-party API, AI/LLM service, or analytics tool ever sees the contents of an export or import file.

d. Profile visibility (three tiers)

Every account holder has a visibility setting: private (the default), public, or the access a connection ("fellow") can be granted on top of either. This setting controls what other people using the Service — or anyone with your profile link — can see at mushin.aqnas.xyz/@{username}. It is separate from, and does not change, the third-party rules in section 4c above: changing your visibility never sends any data to an outside company or AI/LLM service, it only affects what other people on Mushin can see.

  • Private (default): your profile page at /@{username} shows your activity names and their progress and counts to anyone with the link or who finds you through search. It does not show your individual entries or free-text notes. A non-connected visitor cannot open an activity's detail page (/@{username}/{activity}) on a private account — that page redirects back to your profile instead.
  • Public: your entire record — including free-text notes, individual entries, stats, and streaks — is visible to anyone who has your profile link, whether or not they have an account.
  • Connections (fellows): a mutual connection you've separately granted sharing consent to (see "Connections" below) can see your full record, including entries and notes, even while your account is set to private.
  • Switching to public is your own explicit choice. Before it can take effect for the first time, you're shown a one-time screen that plainly explains what each setting shows. You can change this setting again at any time from your account settings, and the change takes effect immediately.
  • If you were already using a private account before this three-tier model took effect, you were shown a one-time notice explaining what changed before your activity names became visible under the new rules.
  • Guest accounts cannot have a public profile. Guests have no username and no /@... page, so this setting does not apply to them.

e. Connections ("fellows")

You can send another account holder a connection request. A connection only becomes mutual — a "fellow" — once the other person accepts it; they can also decline it.

  • Becoming connected does not, by itself, expose your private entries or notes. That requires a separate, explicit sharing consent from you, given through a dedicated consent step that states plainly what you're allowing the other person to see.
  • Either person can remove the connection at any time. Doing so immediately revokes any sharing consent in both directions — your former fellow can no longer see your private entries and notes, and you can no longer see theirs.
  • You can also block another account. Blocking immediately removes any existing connection and sharing access in both directions, and is silent — the blocked person is not notified that they've been blocked.
  • Connection requests, acceptances, and removals generate in-app, content-free notifications only (for example, "so-and-so wants to connect"). We do not send these as email or push notifications, and they never include your entries or notes.

f. Discoverability and search

All accounts — public and private alike — can be found by searching for a username or display name. For a private account, this means your activity names (not your entries or notes) may be visible to someone who finds you this way, the same as if they had visited your profile link directly.

  • Tag search is public-only. Searching by tag only returns activities belonging to public accounts. It cannot return a private or connections-only account, and it never searches the text of your entries or notes.
  • If you've blocked someone, neither of you can find the other through search.

g. The third-party rule still applies, unchanged

Connections and visibility settings only ever make your data visible to other people using Mushin — never to a third-party API. The rule in section 4c above is unchanged by any of this: no entry, note, tag, or match record is ever sent to an outside company or AI/LLM service because of a connection, a visibility setting, or a search.

5. How we delete your information

a. Deletion process

  • When you request account deletion, or when a guest record passes its retention period (section 3), we mark that information for deletion and delete it without delay.

b. Deletion method

  • Electronic records are permanently deleted from our database in a way that cannot be recovered.
  • When an account is deleted, our database's cascading delete (ON DELETE CASCADE) structure removes everything linked to that account — categories, sub-tallies, field definitions, tags, entries, notes, entry values, match records, and connection and block records in both directions (so a deleted account also disappears from any other user's fellows list and pending requests). The design leaves no orphaned data behind, so deletion is complete.
  • Guest data is removed the same way by the automated retention job.

6. Your rights and how to exercise them

  1. You (including guests) can exercise the following rights at any time:
    • Request access to your personal information
    • Request correction of any errors
    • Request deletion
    • Request that we stop processing your data or withdraw consent
  2. How to exercise these rights
    • Delete control in the Service: account holders can delete their account, and guests can delete their own data, directly from within the Service. Doing so triggers the complete deletion described in section 5.
    • By contacting us: for access, correction, or consent withdrawal requests, you can contact the privacy contact listed in section 9 in writing or by email, and we will respond without undue delay.
  3. Guests have these rights too. A guest's data, even without logging in, is personal information stored on our servers, so guests have the same rights as account holders and can delete the data linked to their device cookie directly.
  4. You're responsible for protecting your own personal information and for not including someone else's sensitive information without their consent — particularly in free-text fields like notes.
  5. Exporting and importing your data: from the Service you can download a copy of all your data — including your notes, your connection list, and any pending connection requests — as a file (data portability), and you can upload a previously exported file to replace an account's data with it (for example, to carry your record over to a new account). An uploaded file completely replaces the destination account's existing data, so the Service asks you to confirm before proceeding. Because an exported file may contain personal data (including notes and connections), only import a file that is your own.

7. How we protect your information

We take the following measures to keep your information secure.

  1. Secure password storage — for email/password accounts, passwords are never stored in plain text. They are converted to an irreversible Argon2id hash before storage.
  2. Encryption in transit — all communication is encrypted with HTTPS/TLS, with HSTS and related security policies applied.
  3. Access control and data isolation — all data is isolated by account identifier (owner_id), so you can only access your own data. Access to our servers and database is restricted to the minimum necessary.
  4. Session protection — login sessions are managed via signed cookies with the HttpOnly (blocks script access), Secure (HTTPS only), and SameSite=Lax (mitigates cross-site request forgery) attributes. Authentication information is never stored in browser localStorage.
  5. Data minimization — we collect only the minimum personal information needed to provide the Service (for example, with Google sign-in we request only the openid email profile scope).

8. Cookies

  1. We use the following cookies to keep you signed in and to identify guest sessions:
    • Session/authentication cookie (mushin_session) — a signed cookie that keeps you signed in
    • Guest identifier cookie — links a guest's data to their server account without requiring sign-in
  2. We do not use advertising, tracking, or third-party analytics cookies.
  3. You can refuse cookies through your browser settings, but doing so may limit your ability to stay signed in or use guest mode.
  4. If a guest signs in to an existing account, the cookie that identified their previous session is invalidated and removed, and that guest session can no longer be reused on that device. This prevents a previous user's session from persisting on a shared device.
